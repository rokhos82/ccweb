Sales and Use Taxes

Wyoming has had SALES AND USE TAXES since 1935. Sales taxes apply to the retail sale of personal property or services. The purchaser generally pays the tax, and in the case of vehicle sales, the responsiblity for collecting and remitting the tax is on the County Treasurer.

There are several important things about paying sales tax in Wyoming:

  1. Sales tax must be paid within 45 days of purchase;
  2. If a vehicle is purchased from a dealership, a bill of sale must be presented.
  3. The sales tax rate you pay is always the higher of the rates between where you live and where the vehicle is purchased.

Sales Tax is a tax levied on any sale that takes place in the State of Wyoming.

Use Tax is a tax levied on the sale of any property that takes place outside of the State of Wyoming, for use, storage or consumption in Wyoming.
