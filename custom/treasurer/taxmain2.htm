Wyoming Property Taxes

Wyoming law delegates the task of collecting taxes to the county treasurer. Although it's hard to believe, in a lot of ways the collecting of taxes is a good thing. The taxes fund our hospital and schools, our health department, hospital and airport, and even help build our roads.

Unfortunately, the whole process of determining how much a taxpayer owes and where the money goes can be complicated. Hopefully, the information provided here can help shed some light on the process.

A property tax in Wyoming is an AD VALOREM tax.

In order to tax the property, a TAXABLE VALUE must be determined.

First, the County Assessor assigns it a FAIR MARKET VALUE. The fair market value is multiplied by a TAXATION RATE to calculate the taxable value. The taxation rate depends on how the Assessor classifies the property.

Properties are classified in one of three areas;

Gross production of minerals and mine products;
Property used for industrial purposes; and
All other property, REAL and PERSONAL.
Currently, the taxation rates are:
100% for Gross production of minerals and mine products 11.5% for industrial property
9.5% for all other property

Ad Valorem means "on value," or that the taxes are based on the value of the property.
Real property is land and property permanently attached to the land. Modulars on a foundation, houses and non-removable buildings, for example.
Personal property is movable property - mobile homes, construction equipment or mineral production, for example.
Taxable Value is a percentage of the fair market value.

Currently 100% of the value for mineral production, 11.5% of the value for industrial property and 9.5% for real and personal property.
