Marriage License

Marriage Licenses are valid (in the State of Wyoming only) for a period of one year from the date of issue. No blood test is required. Applicants must be at least 18 years of age to obtain a license without parental consent.   For detailed information on marriage licenses, please read Wyoming State Statutes 20-1-101 through 20-1-113, which is available on the web.

Applicants under the age of 16 must have a court order before a license can be issued.

Before you apply for the license, you and your fiancée need to know and provide the following information:

  • Father's place of birth
  • Mother's place of birth
  • Mother's maiden name
  • If a previous marriage ended in death or divorce, the date of that death or divorce must be known.
  • Bride's and groom's social security numbers. While it is not required, we prefer to have each person provide identification, which shows his/her social security number. This helps to avoid having the wrong information on a legal document, which may cause problems later on.
  • Residential address
  • Age of bride and groom

Rules were adopted by the State of Wy on April 21, 2008 as follows:  “(a) Obtain the information required for the completion of the certificate with the exception of that regarding the ceremony; (b) Secure the signatures of the people to be married on the certificate”. This means that both the bride and groom will be required to sign the application/certificate.
